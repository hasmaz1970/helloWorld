    Executive Summary

    "The success of any business endeavor is ultimately measured in its ability to generate value for the enterprise, either in terms of explicit monetary profit or tangible value which can be translated into monetary profit through a related activity. This is true of a single enterprise or an ecosystem of partners. In the context of ecosystems--a federation of multiple enterprises (e.g. commercial, governmental, charitable, non-profit organizations) – this is further complicated by competing forces and differing priorities of the ecosystem partners, who will each have independent interests outside of the ecosystem. Ref. [1]"

    Figure-1: Why Monetization?

    We are all on the brink of an unprecedented digital revolution that will dramatically change the way we live, interact and how businesses conducted. For the first time in history, the businesses are being challenged with so many drivers of change from different perspectives: new markets, new technology, new business models, new policies, new demands for scale, security and regulation compliancy. Simply, an evolutionary transformation is not enough, and only the businesses that can succeed in such a revolutionary transformation with their core Business and Operational Support Systems (BSS/OSS) will survive.

    A quick reflection on the history, reveals a stunning fact about pace of business change: ~500years of change is LESS THAN ~50years of change is LESS THAN the last ~20years of change

    • 1500s-1950s: First, Second and Third Industrial Revolutions. Cars, Planes, Television, Cameras and Plain old Telephony in our lives.
    • 1950s-2000s: Start of Fourth Industrial Revolution: Internet Born, Personal Computers, Mobile Phones
    • 2000s-2010s: Second wave of Fourth Industrial Revolution: High Speed Broadband Internet, ECommerce Boom, On-Demand High-Definition Content, 3G/4G Internet and Mobility Everywhere
    • 2010s-NEXT: Next wave of Fourth Industrial Revolution: Cloud Computing, Big Data, Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence based Analytics, Prediction, Internet of Things (IoT) and 5G enabling: Mobile Broadband, Massive Machine to Machine and Ultra Reliable Communication Infrastructure everywhere

    Especially the following areas of technology will play a significant role and drive new digital business ecosystems:

    • 5G allowing ground breaking capabilities for next generation apps and services anytime, anywhere providing three types of specialized connectivity: high-throughput broadband access (10Gbps), ultra reliable low latency networks (1ms) and massive machine type communications allowing millions of devices on a small area (1km2)
    • IoT (Internet of Things) technologies allowing many different types of appliances, devices and items connected to the global Internet
    • SDN (Software Defined Networking) / NFV (Network Function Virtualization) / Cloud technologies allowing intent based fully controllable networks, virtual infrastructures that can be dynamically scaled, load-balanced across disperse geographical locations and distributed physical infrastructures; all of which making it possible to dynamically orchestrate, on-the-fly deploy and scale the entire lifecycle of apps and network functions accessible anywhere, anytime
    • Blockchain technology allowing digital contracts (Smart Contracts) revolutionizing both identity and trust management

    This new digital business ecosystems paradigm is expected to dominate as new offerings are curated, combining products from multiple service providers or from adjacent sectors involving many business partners to create new propositions for customers and leverage value-added up-sell for service providers. These ecosystems will inevitably create greater complexity in managing the commercial relationship within the ecosystem - recognizing that each partner will have their own business processes and systems managing their enterprise.

    The following diagram depicts some of the realities of the precedented complexities and its impacts on monetization.

    Figure-2: New Digital Business Ecosystems

    "The business-to-consumer (B2C) world has been transformed by social media and commerce platforms, enabled by service providers’ multi-billion-dollar investments in global communication networks. However, as mobile data has enjoyed a compound annual growth rate of 66 percent for the last five years, research by Accenture for the World Economic Forum shows that the share of profits going to the owners and operators of these networks has declined from 60 percent a decade ago to 45 percent today. Ubiquitous connectivity has driven down prices, eroded profit margins and lengthened the time it takes for new infrastructure to pay back, which is slowing the innovation cycle. Ref. [2]".

    On the business landscape, today's service providers must not only compete with Google, Amazon or Apple, but also must figure out how to transform their as-is business to platform-based ecosystems or how to actively participate on other ecosystems like Amazon's or Google's ecosystems. Plus, with the new business potential driven by 5G and IOT, service providers must also partner and be part of Independent Software Vendor, Automobile, Equipment supplier and alike ecosystems.

    On the technology landscape, all businesses want to become operationally more agile and cost effective taking advantage of the new technological advancements such as SDN/NFV combined with a move to Cloud computing. These transformations, meanwhile providing agility and scalability, are changing the fundamental infrastructures of businesses as well as introducing new partners and cost centers to be considered within the monetization processes.

    Important

    As a consequence, the rise of e-commerce, communication technologies and the advent of the digital economy blended with the new operational models are profoundly changing how businesses think and operate. Today’s B2B and B2C customers expect products and services to be delivered and billed on demand – in minutes, rather than days and weeks. Today's B2B partners expect new multi-party business alliance and partnership models to be modeled and established immediately with relevant offerings to be launched on commercial sales catalog in hours, rather than weeks.

    Thus, for the digital service providers that aim to generate new revenue streams, fully modeling, orchestrating and sharing the revenue among business partners, the necessity for a next generation Monetization Platform is inevitable.

    The aforementioned mesh of business drivers necessitate: dramatically agile, business flexible, operationally scalable, ready to integrate Monetization Platform for the next generation digital service provider IT systems.

    What's MonE?

    MonE is an on/off-premise cloud native Monetization Platform by PiA. MonE enables transformation from the current state of C/B&RM (Charging, Billing and Revenue Management) systems to future mode of Monetization Platform in which:

    • The current state of C&B represents proliferated and segregated silos of billing systems, either monolithic or old-style layered architectures with standards/processes developed to charge and bill for events generated through physical network elements as well as for events on product/service orders generated via Order Management systems, without any consolidated capabilities for complex pricing, charging methods, partner billing and revenue sharing models.

    • The future mode of Monetization Platform represents a distributed, flexible monetization system comprised of lego-approach service based components. Enabled by MonE, this next generation Monetization Platform considers technology impacts of IoT, 5G, SDN/NFV and Cloud on your business whether be Telecom, Energy, Utility of Medical Billing based, as well as providing capabilities for managing multiple revenue streams, charging, pricing, payment methods and revenue sharing of complex partner ecosystems.

    The result is an on/off premise cloud-native flexible model-driven system in which enterprise's business, finance and partner strategies being modeled in MonE Monetization Template Models and MonE Tariff, Discount and Balance/Quota Model Packages; MonE Platform executes these models for managing the converged B2B and B2C charging, billing, settlement and revenue sharing journey of the enterprise.
